SAKURA TREES AT PAOAY, ATOK, BENGUET

Edward Haight and Japanese Hirmo Yoshikawa established the Benguet Kochi Sisterhood Park in June 2016 at Paoay, Atok, Benguet. Thirty Sakura trees were planted. The Japanese experts say that Sakura trees will bear flowers in three to five years.
According to Edward Haight, the owner of the land, the Yoshikawa team tried to seek other areas in the province like Santo Thomas, Madaymen, Paoay and Mankayan to see the climate if it is appropriate for the Sakura trees. But after several surveys, the team came back to the Paoay because they found the climate is suitable for the sakura trees. The sakura trees grows in cold place just like in Japan that they have winter season. The land is 3.3 hectares.
Haight said that there are two varities of the Sakura trees planted in the park-the Yokiwara Sakura, which produce pink blossoms and Sindaya sakura, which produce white blossoms. The Sakura will be fully matured in five years. Currently, the Sakura trees are only 2 years old.
Irrigation and water system was already done. The park was funded by the Japanese team.
"Visitors are welcome but the park is currently under renovation. We will still be landscaping the area. The parking area, comfort room and the pasalubong center are still to be constructed. The cottage is also under construction, Haight said."
Soon, the Sisterhood park of Paoay will become a national park. The park is 1.6 kilometers away from the Halsema Highway and around 54 kilometers away from Baguio City.

-An interview from Mr. Edward Haight
